Ben Nix-Bradley

BIO

  Ben is a multidisciplinary composer and artist from Los Angeles California. With a background in digital art and software development, he combines deep technological experience with formal composition training to provide substantial assistance and proficiency in a variety of professional entertainment environments. He is an accomplished student and has won several awards an honors within the Los Angeles Community College District and California State University. Including the Igor Paltsev, and Jerry & Carol Goldsmith Scholarships for excellence in composition and academic performance.

  In 2021 he graduated from the Los Angeles City College - Herb Alpert Music Center with highest distinction as a Herb Alpert Scholar and Ralph Bunche Scholar. Ben proceeded to California State University Northridge as a Junior in Commercial and Media Composition for the class of 2023. In 2022 he was awarded the Mark Brunner Scholarship by the Professional Audio Manufactures Alliance. Ben continues to broaden his experience as a composer and educator by tutoring in music technology and providing workshops to the CSUN community through the Creative Maker Studio at the University Library.

  With a wide range of credits from voice acting, video editing, animation, and composition. Ben has a wealth of experience in both linear and interactive story telling. Recent software development efforts include an interactive music player for table-top role playing games and a virtual reality training environment for first year nursing students that was recently adopted by the university.

  Currently Ben is focused on writing music for a variety of short films and exploring potential careers in education and music therapy before setting his sights on graduate school. He looks forward to opportunities to perform and compose new and meaningful work for commercial, educational and concert settings.
